## Design Context

### Users
Mobile-first crypto-native players on Farcaster (MiniKit frames) and MiniPay (Celo wallet). They discover Waffles through social feeds, play during downtime, and are motivated by real money prizes. Context: quick sessions, phone in one hand, often distracted. Frequency: 3x/week (Mon/Wed/Fri game schedule).

### Brand Personality
**Urgent. Arcade. Unfiltered.** The vibe is "fight night ticket sales meets retro arcade" — not polished fintech, not casual mobile game. The roast messages during gameplay set the tone: bold, competitive, zero corporate polish.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme:** Dark — players are on phones at night, between activities
- **Palette:** Gold (money/reward), Cyan (brand accent), Red/warm (urgency/scarcity), near-black surfaces with noise texture
- **Typography:** Custom pixel fonts (EditUndo, Brockmann, N-DOT47) — arcade cabinet energy
- **Motion:** Spring physics, fast taps, tension animations. Not smooth/elegant — snappy/responsive
- **Anti-references:** Clean fintech apps, friendly pastel UIs, anything that says "we're a safe responsible platform"

### Design Principles
1. **Scarcity drives action.** Every screen communicates "this is running out." Spots left > player count. Countdown > description.
2. **One tap to buy.** Remove every step between "I want in" and money leaving the wallet. No modals, no tier selection, no confirmation screens.
3. **The game sells itself.** Don't explain the game — let people play a demo question. Experience > instruction.
4. **Notifications are the funnel.** Aggressive, escalating, scarcity-aware. Every notification includes spots remaining. Copy gets more urgent as time runs out.
5. **Regret is retention.** "SOLD OUT" notifications to non-buyers. Show them what they missed. Drive notification opt-in for next time.

## Conversion Funnel Brief

### Workstream 1: Onboarding Redesign
**Goal:** Sell the app in 3 slides, end with a buy CTA.

- **Slide 1:** The pitch — one headline ("GUESS THE SCENE. WIN THE POT."), live countdown to next game, spots remaining if tickets are open
- **Slide 2:** Play a demo question — keep the existing interactive demo (strongest conversion tool), add confetti on correct
- **Slide 3:** The payoff — "You got it right. Now imagine winning $63." → shows live game card with spots remaining + 1-tap buy CTA
- Shows once per user, then lobby is the recurring experience

### Workstream 2: Game Card Scarcity Overhaul
**Goal:** Make "X SPOTS LEFT" the dominant information on the game card.

- **Primary element:** "X SPOTS LEFT" — large, color-shifts based on fill percentage:
  - >50% remaining: white/neutral
  - 17-50% remaining: gold, subtle pulse
  - 3-17% remaining: red, aggressive pulse, "ALMOST GONE"
  - 0 remaining: "SOLD OUT" locked state
- **Secondary element:** Prize pool amount (smaller, below spots)
- **Remove:** Player count, player avatars, "X players joined" — scarcity replaces social proof
- **CTA:** "BUY TICKET — $X" button. 1 tap fires wallet transaction directly.

### Workstream 3: 1-Tap Purchase Flow
**Goal:** Delete the BuyTicketModal. Buying happens inline on the game card.

- Tap "BUY TICKET — $X" → wallet popup (MetaMask/MiniPay) → confirm → success toast
- Delete free ticket tier entirely
- Delete BuyTicketModal and PurchaseView components
- Button states: default → spinner → "YOU'RE IN ✓" (success) / "TRY AGAIN" (error)
- Error recovery inline: "Transaction cancelled. Tap to try again." / "Not enough USDC. Add funds →"
- Sold out state: "SOLD OUT" disabled button + "Get notified for next game" link

### Workstream 4: Notification Copy Rewrite
**Goal:** Every notification escalates urgency and includes spots remaining.

- Keep all countdown intervals (24h → 1m) and all ticket-open intervals
- Rewrite copy to include scarcity data and escalate tone:
  - Early: "Waffles #XXX just opened. 30 spots. $63 pot. Go."
  - Mid: "18 spots left for Waffles #XXX. Clock's ticking."
  - Late: "6 spots left. This fills up every time."
  - Final: "2 SPOTS LEFT for Waffles #XXX. Last chance."
- Add "SOLD OUT" regret notification to non-buyers
- Use percentage-based scarcity thresholds (adapts to variable ticket caps)
- "Flipped" notifications during live game — keep and make more provocative
